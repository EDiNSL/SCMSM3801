The Myth of the 6-incher

On men’s long lasting desire for length

Our story began in 1948.

Alfred Kinsey and their colleagues asked a bunch of people to measure their own erect penises and mail the result to them (Kinsey, Pomeroy, & Martin, 1948).

They actually didn't reveal the data. Someone in the Kinsey institute then just scoop up the descriptive summary, and then conclude the mean of 2770 hard white phallus data is 15.77cm (6.21 inches).

And then the scientific community rode on it. Some might even call it the dawn of the 6-incher myth, and there comes a floodgate of studies with self-report data that claims to back it up (King, 2020).

And some racists are loving the sex party

In 2013, Richard Lynn took an affection on a theory called the Rushton’s r–K Life History Theory, which basically goes like this:
1. How advance a race is depends on how much they reproduce.
2. The more you reproduce, the more horomones you have.
3. The more horomones you have, the larger your penis is.

To prove that, Lynn resort to the most scientific source possible; he went on everyoneweb(dot)com and press Ctrl C+P on the data in 2012, which listed the average of penis sizes per country.

Problem is, most of the data relies on self-report studies mentioned by King (2020) (McGreal, 2012). Unfortunately for once these phallus fit into Lynn's mind almost perfectly, and they conclude that given how blacks have apparently the largest penises, followed by whites and asians, this means hte blacks are lacking behind in civilization ... somehow.

Collective orgasm

So now we have two raging hard conceptions begging for validation -
1. The average erect penis size is about 16cm.
2. Penis sizes varies by race.

And the Internet, which has a track record for being a beacon of reason, is loving it.

BuzzFeed was calling it "The infographic of the year so far." The Sun, The Independent, and countless news organizations shown up in a simple google search for penises in 2012 all took a feel of the penetrating conclusion.

Growing fallacy under scrutiny

In 2015, Veale and their team scrutinized 96 literatures on investigating average penis sizes and select them through a list of standardized criterias, which for our concerns include -
- penis size was measured by a health professional
- at least 50 participants, all are at least 17 years old
- a mean and SD of the sample size is provided
- erect and flaccid penises is measured from the root (pushed to the bone) to the tip
- published in English
In the end, only 20 studies came out from the scrutiny.

More recently in 2020, King did a similar analysis and came up with 21 studies, but they also include 10 studies with self-report data which are most often cited, including by which the 2012 dataset (and by extension Lynn's study) is based on.

So now we have scientists basically doing verfication and fact checking on phallus claims. What do these data tell us?

So what is the average size, really?

Both King and Veale's studies as well as this visualization has shown that the average size is around the 13cm ballpark.

While it is not enough to prove that there is absolutely no difference in sizes by country, it is already enough to disprove attempts to affirm correlation between race and gential sizes.

The bigger question therefore is why, according to King (2020), do the myths linger on? Now that we know the origins and motivations of fallible information, there still seems to be a disconnect on a sociological level that make these scientific knowledge hard to penetrate into the public consciousness. What makes people so compelled to bluff about their bodies? We suggest that culture might have something to do with this, and King (2020) summarized a host of possibe factors, including pornography, media, and gender constructs.

Until we get a clearer picture, it seems like men's obsession with their private parts is going to last longer than they would like, for once.
